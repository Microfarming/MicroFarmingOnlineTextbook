General Recommendations

Transplanting: Should be done early in the morning, finishing before 9 AM, or in the afternoon, starting after 3:30 PM.

Loss Review: Check for losses in the first 5 days to replace them.

Watering: For the first 2 weeks, water 3 times a day to reach field capacity (adequate moisture). From the 3rd week, water twice a day, monitoring the moisture to maintain uniform moisture. During the fruiting stage, water 3 times a day again to maintain uniform moisture.

Pest and Disease Monitoring: Daily monitoring to identify and report pests and diseases, as open field cultivation is prone to be affected at any time.

Drip Tape Review: Ensure drip tapes are in the correct position, with the dripper facing up and checking for leaks.

Mulch Plastic Review: Ensure mulch plastic is tight enough to avoid hot air currents that can damage the roots internally.

Lettuce and Cabbage Harvesting: Activate watering 1 hour before harvesting to prevent crop dehydration.

Basket Use: Do not use field baskets for final product shipment.

Traceability Control: Keep records to demonstrate traceability, including germination date, transplant date, products applied for any disease or pest, harvest start and end dates, visit records, labeling of gardens with crop names and transplant dates.

Climate Change Adaptation: Construction of special roofs for plant protection against rain and high temperatures (optional based on monetary capital for investment). Estimated time for 120 linear meters of any crop is approximately 24 hours.

Physical Control for Pests: Use physical controllers such as natural and artificial barriers. Estimated time for 120 linear meters of any crop is approximately 24 hours.

Chili

Characteristics
Germination: 9 days, 3.7 units
Transplant: 25 days
Plant Spacing: 30 cm
# Plants (m): 3.33 plants
Harvest: 70 days
Rows: 1 row

Transplanting Activities:

Estimated time for 120 linear meters: 4 hours.
Check drip tape alignment for plant protection and irrigation efficiency.
Moisture review to ensure adequate field capacity (soil moisture).
Preparation before transplanting: Each hole where the seedling will be placed should have organic fertilizer and humic acids.
Transplant: Make the hole the appropriate size to fit the seedling and cover without pressing the stem.
Watering after transplanting (1 hour).

Staking:

Estimated time for 120 linear meters: 48 hours.
Stakes are the bases where the ropes that will support the plants are tied to prevent breakage from wind, weight, rain, etc. They should be 2.5 meters apart and 2.30 meters high.

Tying:

Estimated time for 120 linear meters: 12 hours.
When plants reach approximately 20 centimeters, they should be tied for protection, avoiding damage and losses.

Pruning:

When the crop starts to flower, formative pruning should be done, starting to induce 3 axes to achieve different levels at harvest time.

Harvesting:

Estimated start: Approximately 70 days after transplanting.
First two weeks: Estimated time for 120 linear meters is 2 hours.
Subsequent weeks: Estimated time for 120 linear meters is 4 hours, as production increases.

Cilantro (Coriander)

Characteristics
Germination: 7 days, 80 units
Transplant: Not applicable
Plant Spacing: 15 cm
# Plants (m): 6.67 plants
Harvest: 50 days
Rows: 1 row

Sowing:

Sow cilantro seeds directly into well-drained soil, about 0.5 cm deep. Keep soil consistently moist until germination.

Thinning:

Once seedlings are 5-7 cm tall, thin them to 15 cm apart to allow for proper growth and development.

Watering:

Maintain even moisture throughout the growing period to prevent bolting (premature flowering).

Harvesting:

Harvest leaves when they are young and tender, typically around 50 days after sowing. Use sharp scissors to cut leaves just above the soil level.
